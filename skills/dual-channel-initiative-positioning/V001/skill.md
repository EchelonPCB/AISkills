# Objective

Construct a controlled narrative that allows an operator to present initiatives originating outside formal institutions (e.g., clubs, independent systems, external pipelines) while reinforcing—not undermining—their institutional role. The output is a structured positioning strategy that aligns execution with authority and enables scaling through institutional channels.

---

# Trigger

Use this skill when:

- Holding meetings with leadership (e.g., deans, directors, executives)
- Managing dual roles (institutional position + independent execution)
- Presenting initiatives that do not require institutional permission but benefit from institutional scaling
- Avoiding perception of bypassing authority structures
- Aligning fast-moving systems with slower governance bodies

---

# Do Not Use When

- The initiative exists purely within institutional scope
- There is no external or independent execution layer
- The audience does not control or influence institutional scaling
- The goal is informal discussion rather than strategic positioning

---

# Required Inputs

1. Institutional role (e.g., senator, representative, manager)
2. External initiatives (e.g., projects, pipelines, systems)
3. Stakeholder (e.g., dean, executive, advisor)
4. Meeting objective (alignment, support, validation, scaling)

---

# Optional Inputs

1. Supporting proof (awards, completed systems, metrics)
2. External partnerships or pipelines
3. Long-term vision (e.g., scaling into events, programs, infrastructure)
4. Known institutional priorities

---

# Outputs

1. Structured narrative flow
2. Role-safe positioning language
3. Initiative introduction strategy
4. Institutional alignment framing
5. Controlled escalation pathway

# Procedure

## 1. Anchor Institutional Authority

1.1 Begin by reinforcing your formal role  
1.2 Reference active responsibilities (e.g., engaging reps, collecting issues)  
1.3 Establish legitimacy within the system  

---

## 2. Introduce Execution Layer

2.1 Transition to initiatives already in motion  
2.2 Present them as validated (not hypothetical)  
2.3 Avoid framing them as dependent on the institution  

---

## 3. Bridge the Two Layers

3.1 Explicitly connect external work to institutional relevance  
3.2 Use language:
> “I see my role as bridging these efforts so they can scale within [institution]”

3.3 Eliminate any implication of bypassing the system  

---

## 4. Frame as Scalable Infrastructure

4.1 Reposition initiatives from “projects” to “systems”  
4.2 Emphasize repeatability and institutional value  
4.3 Avoid student/club framing  

---

## 5. Introduce Advanced Concepts Carefully

5.1 Do not lead with large-scale ideas (e.g., competitions, expansions)  
5.2 Introduce them as:
> “One direction this could evolve into…”

5.3 Observe stakeholder reaction before expanding  

---

## 6. Align With Institutional Priorities

6.1 Tie initiative to:
- student outcomes  
- external engagement  
- institutional visibility  

6.2 Let stakeholder validate alignment  

---

## 7. Extract Institutional Pathway

7.1 Ask:
- Where does this fit?  
- What would need to be true to support it?  

7.2 Identify next step:
- introduction  
- pilot  
- follow-up meeting  

---

# Decision Logic

- IF initiative appears independent, THEN reinforce bridge to institution  
- IF stakeholder shows interest, THEN expand scope gradually  
- IF stakeholder is skeptical, THEN retreat to proven execution layer  
- IF role authority is questioned, THEN re-anchor in institutional function  
- IF initiative is too large, THEN present as future direction, not current ask  

---

# Validation

- Narrative does not undermine institutional role  
- External initiatives are framed as assets, not alternatives  
- Stakeholder understands scaling opportunity  
- At least one follow-on action is identified  
- No language suggests bypassing governance  

---

# Rules

- Never say “sidestep” or “bypass” institutional systems  
- Never frame initiatives as competing with your role  
- Always present execution as already validated  
- Always maintain control of narrative escalation  
- Always leave with a defined next step  

---

# Failure Modes

**Failure:** Appears to ignore institutional role  
→ **Fix:** Re-anchor in responsibilities immediately  

**Failure:** Initiative sounds like a club idea  
→ **Fix:** Reframe as system/infrastructure  

**Failure:** Stakeholder disengages  
→ **Fix:** Shift to alignment questions  

**Failure:** Overexposure of large vision too early  
→ **Fix:** Reposition as long-term direction  

---

# Dependencies

- None required, but benefits from:
  - stakeholder research
  - prior execution proof
  - meeting preparation frameworks

---

# Assumptions

- Stakeholder has authority or influence over institutional alignment  
- Operator has at least one validated execution example  
- Meeting has a defined objective beyond introduction
