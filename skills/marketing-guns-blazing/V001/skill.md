# Objective

Deploy a high-intensity marketing system that rapidly generates, filters, and converts student leads into active contributors while minimizing wasted effort on low-commitment participants.

---

# Trigger

Use this skill when:

- an organization needs rapid membership growth
- recruitment must prioritize **quality over quantity**
- manpower is limited and requires efficient filtering
- a project requires committed contributors, not passive members

---

# Do Not Use When

Do not use this skill when:

- the goal is broad awareness or branding only
- participation is intended to be low-commitment or social
- the organization cannot support onboarding or task assignment
- no structured follow-up system exists

---

# Required Inputs

1. Defined project or organization value proposition
2. Interest form or lead capture system
3. Communication platform (e.g., Discord)
4. At least one recurring meeting (GBM or equivalent)

---

# Optional Inputs

- Physical marketing materials (posters)
- Existing social media presence
- Committee structure for delegation
- Predefined onboarding tasks

---

# Outputs

- Qualified lead pool
- Filtered active members
- Identified high-potential contributors
- Initial leadership pipeline candidates

---

# Support Layers

- Outreach layer: in-person engagement
- Funnel layer: interest form filtering
- Conversion layer: meetings and tasks
- Retention layer: committees and ownership
- Evaluation layer: participation tracking

---

# Procedure

## Phase 1 — Visibility Injection

1. Conduct direct, in-person outreach using short pitch:
   - Introduce project in under 15 seconds
   - Capture contact immediately
   - Avoid extended explanation

2. Deploy a single high-clarity poster:
   - One core message
   - QR code to interest form
   - Minimal text

3. Target high-traffic academic areas:
   - Engineering buildings
   - Study areas
   - Between-class transitions

---

## Phase 2 — Lead Filtering

1. Route all contacts to an interest form
2. Include filtering questions:
   - motivation
   - contribution intent
   - time commitment

3. Disqualify low-effort responses
4. Admit only serious candidates into onboarding channel

---

## Phase 3 — Controlled Onboarding

1. Place all new members in restricted access environment
2. Require:
   - attendance at one meeting
   - completion of one small task

3. Grant deeper access only after requirements met

---

## Phase 4 — Conversion Events

1. Structure meetings to:
   - show real work or output
   - define current scope clearly
   - offer one actionable entry point

2. Avoid:
   - long explanations
   - abstract discussions
   - open-ended brainstorming

---

## Phase 5 — Task Assignment

1. Assign small, real tasks:
   - research
   - documentation
   - simple execution

2. Do not assign roles before task completion
3. Track completion and responsiveness

---

## Phase 6 — Leader Identification

1. Identify individuals who:
   - complete tasks without reminders
   - attend consistently
   - demonstrate initiative

2. Promote these individuals into:
   - committee roles
   - ownership responsibilities

---

# Decision Logic

| Condition | Action |
|----------|--------|
| high enthusiasm, low follow-through | deprioritize |
| consistent task completion | promote |
| repeated absence | remove from active pool |
| high engagement + initiative | consider leadership track |
| unclear motivation | filter out |

---

# Validation

This skill is successful when:

- leads convert into active contributors
- at least 20–30% of recruits complete tasks
- a subset of members attends multiple sessions
- leadership candidates emerge organically

---

# Rules

- prioritize action over explanation
- do not chase uninterested individuals
- do not assign leadership without proof of execution
- maintain strict scope communication
- enforce contribution before access

---

# Failure Modes

| Failure | Cause | Recovery |
|--------|------|----------|
| high turnout, low retention | weak filtering | tighten form and onboarding |
| burnout of leadership | over-centralization | delegate earlier |
| low engagement | unclear value proposition | refine pitch |
| chaotic onboarding | no structure | enforce phased access |

---

# Dependencies

- consistent meeting cadence
- functioning communication platform
- ability to assign and track tasks
- leadership discipline in enforcement

---

# Assumptions

- not all recruits are intended to stay
- filtering is necessary for sustainability
- contributors are more valuable than participants
- leadership must emerge through action, not assignment
